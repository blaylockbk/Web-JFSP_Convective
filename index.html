HRRR Model Forecasts of Convective Outflows in the Fire Environment

We are investigating the impacts of convective outflows on fire behavior. The ability of the High Resolution Rapid Refresh (HRRR) model to forecast the characteristics of such mesoscale atmospheric boundaries is being evaluated in the continental U.S. and Alaska.

Can the HRRR facilitate nowcasting at lead teams less than 6 h and improve situational awareness regarding convective outflows at lead times less than 24 h? While it may not be possible to expect high forecast accuracy for the timing, speed, and intensity of individual convective outflows, there is potential to improve the utility of model output for convective outflow forecasting at lead times of less than 24 h, as well as how the risks associated with such events can be communicated operationally to the fire weather community. This collaborative project between the University of Utah and University of Alaska-Fairbanks also involves operational personnel to help design the study and evaluate the results.
